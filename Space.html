The universe is so immense that it is estimated to be about 93 billion light-years in diameter, containing billions of galaxies, each with billions of stars.

Absence of Atmosphere: In space, there is no atmosphere, which means that sound cannot travel. This is why space is considered a silent vacuum, and astronauts use radios to communicate while in space.
